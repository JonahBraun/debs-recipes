Salmon:   
 3 tbs lemon juice

3 tbs olive oil

 2 tsp sea salt 2 tsp ground black pepper
 6 salmon fillets (6 oz each)1/4 cup olive oil

 Marinate salmon with above ingredients.  While salmon is marinating, prepare leeks and chambord sauce.  Then barbecue to desired doneness and serve with leeks and sauce immediately.  Alternatively, remove salmon from marinade. Pat dry, then dredge in 1/4 cup flour.  Pan fry to desired doneness.

  
 Stewed leeks:
 
 1/2 cup butter   10 cups leeks
 5 tbs white wine
 1 tsp minced garlic
 1 1/2 cups chicken stock
 1/2 cup creme fraiche
 3 tbs chopped parsley
 1 1/2 tbs lemon juice
 3/4 tsp tabasco
 salt to taste

 Remove uppermost green portion from the leeks.  Cut leeks in half lengthwise, then cut into thin, half-moon slices.  Place in colander and rinse well.
Melt butter in large pot.  Add leeks, white wine, salt and garlic.  Cook over low heat for approximately 10 minutes. Stir in creme fraiche, chopped parsley,
lemon juice and tabasco.

 
 Cornstarch thickener:  
 mix 1 tbs water with 1 tbs cornstarch
 
 Sweet & Sour Chambord Sauce
 
 1 tbs oil
 2 tbs scallions, sliced
 1 tbs garlic, minced
 3/4 cup chicken stock
 1/2 cup chambord
 3 tbs rice vinegar
 2 tsp soy sauce
 2 tsp honey
 pinch black pepper
 2 tbs ginger
 Heat oil.  Add scallions, garlic and ginger.  Cook for 20 seconds. Do not brown.  Add chicken stock, chambord, rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey and pepper.  Cook over low heat for 2 minutes.  Stir in cornstarch thickener; cook for an additional 2 minutes.    Strain through a fine mesh strainer.   Can be made ahead.
