--- layout: recipe title: Scalloped Potatoes With Goat Cheese category: sides ---

This recipe reminds me of the scalloped potatoes we had years ago on the farm.  The goats cream cheese adds a subtle sharpness to the full creamy taste.

3 lb yellow-fleshed boiling potatoes
2 - 3  shallots, chopped
2 tbs unsalted butter (or 1 tbs oil and 1 tbs butter)
1 cup skim or 1% milk
1 cup heavy cream
2 oz soft mild goat cheese
6 oz goat feta cheese (crumbled)
1 tbs flour
1 1/2 to 2 tsp salt
1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
Garnish: fresh chives (preferably flowering) snipped with scissors
 

Preheat oven to 350°F.

Peel potatoes and cut into 1/8-inch-thick slices. Keep slices in a large bowl of cold water while making sauce.

Cook shallots in butter in a small skillet over moderately low heat, stirring, until softened. Turn down heat and stir in goat cheese until melted.  Mix flour and milk together and add to pot.  Add cream, feta, salt to taste, and pepper. Bring mixture to a simmer.

Drain potatoes in a large colander and pat dry. Arrange in a buttered 3-quart shallow baking dish in 3 layers, pouring some of sauce over each layer (use all of sauce). Bake, covered with foil, in lower third of oven. Remove foil after 45 minutes and continue to bake until potatoes are tender, 30 to 45 minutes more. If desired, briefly broil potatoes under preheated broiler until top is golden.
