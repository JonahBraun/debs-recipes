--- layout: recipe title: Chocolate Beet Cake with Beet Dust category: desserts --- - 1 3/4 c all purpose flour

- 1 1/2 tsp baking soda

- 1/4 tsp salt

- 2 medium beets, baked, juices reserved or 1 can (15 oz) beets, drained, juice reserved

- 1 1/4 c sugar

1 c vegetable oil

1/2 c juice from beets

3 large eggs

1 tsp vanilla extract

3 squares unsweetened chocolate, melted

1 c chocolate chips, white or dark

 

To bake beets:

 Wash and wrap snugly in aluminum foil.  Bake in a 350 F oven until soft.  Unwrap, pouring any juices into a cup  Reserve.  You'll need 1/2 cup of beet liquid, so if you have just a little beet juice, top it up with water.  Slip skins off and roughly dice beets into food processor.

 

Preheat oven to 350 F.  Arrange rack in the center of the oven.  Coat two 9 inch layer pans of a 9 x 13 inch baking pan with non stick spray. Line the pans with parchment.

Measure flour, baking soda and salt into a medium bowl. Stir to combine and set aside.  

In food processor, puree drained/baked beets and spon into a large bowl.  Add sugar, vegetable oil, and 1/2 c reserved beet juice to the pureed beets and mix on meduim speed until combined.  Add eggs and vanilla extract, blending until completely incorporated.

Add flour mixture to the beet mixture.  Mix on medium speed about two minutes, or until combined.  Add melted unsweetened chocolate.  Mix again on medium speed until combined, adding chocolate chips.

Spoon into pan.  Bake for 25-30 minutes for round layers, about 35 for rectangular cake, until toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.  Let cool toroom temperature before removing from the pans.  The rectangular cake may be cut in half.

 

Fluffy Seven-Minute Icing:

 

1/4 c golden syrup

3/4 c sugar

1/2 tsp cream of tartar

1 egg white

1 tsp vanilla

1/2 c boiling water

 

Pour syrup into a deep bowl.  Add sugar, cream of tartar, egg white and vanilla.  Stir once with a rubber spatula.  Pour boiling water into the bowl all at once, and immediately begin beating with an electric mixer.  Beat until the icing is thick and fluffy, between seven and 10 minutes.  Spread between layers of cake and on top and sides.

 

Beet Dust:

 

1/2 c soarse sanding sugar

few drops beet juice

 

 Place sugar in a bowl and drop in a very small amount of beet juice.  Toss quickly, and spread it on waxed paper to dry.  Sprinkle beet dust over the icing
