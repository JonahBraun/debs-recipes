--- layout: recipe title: Chicken Cacciatore with Portobello Mushrooms category: poultry ---

6 servings

 

8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighns, trimmed of excess skin and fat

salt and pepper to taste

1 tsp. olive oil

1 medium onion, chopped

3 medium portobello mushrooms, stems and gills removed, diced into 3/4 inch cubes

4 cloves of garlic minced

2 tbs flour

1 1/2 cups dry red wine

1/2 cup chicken broth

1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained

2 tsp. minced fresh thyme leaves

1 Parmesan cheese rind

2 tsp. minced fresh sage leaves

 

Preheat oven to 325 F.  Season chicken with salt and pepper.  Heat olive oil in an ovenproof skillet over medium high heat.  Add 4 chicken thighs, skin side down.  Cook without moving until skin is crisp and well browned, about 5 minutes.  Turn and brown second side, about 5 minutes longer.  Transfer to a large plate and repeat with remaining chicken.  Set chicken aside.  When cooled, remove and discard skin.

With a spoon, remove and disard all but 1 tbs. fat from pan.  Add onion, mushrooms, and 1/2 tsp salt to the skillet.  Saute over medium high heat, stirring occasionally, until the moisture evaporates and the vegetables begin to brown, about 6-8 minutes.  Add garlic and saute until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

Stir in the flour and cook, stirring constantly, about 1 minutes.  Add wine, scraping the pan with a wooden spoon to loosen the browned bits.  Stir in broth, tomatoes, thyme, Parmesan rind and pepper to taste.  Add chicken pieces and accumulated juices, submerging chicken in the liquid.  Bring to a simmer, cover and place pot in oven.  Bake about 40 minutes or until chicken is done.

Remove Parmesan rind and chicken pieces.   Bring the liquid to a boil and cook down about 5 minutes to thicken.  Stir in sage.  Taste and adjust with salt and pepper.  The chicken can be taken off the bone and put back into the sauce or served whole.
