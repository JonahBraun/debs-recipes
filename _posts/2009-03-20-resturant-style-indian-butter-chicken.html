This butter chicken tastes just like the kind you get at an Indian restaurant - only better!

1 cup plain yogurt
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 piece (1/2-inch) ginger root, minced
1/2 tbsp Garam masala
1/2 tbsp lime juice
1/2 tbsp medium curry paste
1 tsp mild paprika
1 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp coriander
1/4 tsp salt
4 chicken breasts, with wings if desired

Butter Sauce:
1 tbsp vegetable oil
1 onion, chopped
1 tbsp Garam masala
2 tsp mild paprika
2 tsp medium curry paste
1 can tomatoes
3/4 cup whipping cream
1/3 cup cold butter, cubed
1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Place yogurt in large bowl. Whisk in garlic, ginger, garam masala, lime juice, curry paste, paprika, cumin, coriander and salt.

Add chicken to bowl and turn to coat; cover and refrigerate for 24 hours, turning occasionally. Place chicken in medium roasting pan, cover and roast in 400°F oven until no longer pink inside, about 30 minutes (or more if breasts have bone in them). Transfer to platter and cover with foil, leaving leftover marinade in pan.

Meanwhile, in large skillet or saucepan, heat oil over medium heat. Fry onion until browned, about 10 minutes. Add garam masala, paprika and curry paste; cook, stirring often, until fragrant and beginning to stick to pan, about 5 minutes. Add tomatoes and scrape up brown bits from bottom of pan; mash tomatoes. Simmer until spoon or spatula pulled across bottom of pan leaves gap that fills in slowly, about 15 minutes. Let cool slightly. In food processor or blender, purée until smooth. (This sauce can be made ahead: Let cool; cover and refrigerate for up to 24 hours.)

Pour sauce back into saucepan along with leftover marinade from roasting pan; bring to boil over medium heat. Add cream and butter, a few pieces at a time, cooking just until each is melted. Pour over chicken. Garnish with cilantro.
